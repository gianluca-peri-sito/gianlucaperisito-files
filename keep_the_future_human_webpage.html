AI Is Probably Not What You Think

How the reality of AI is honestly kind of creepy
By Gianluca Peri | Autumn 2025 | Keep The Future Human

Brains Inside Computers

Modern Artificial Intelligence products, such as ChatGPT or Gemini, are not normal computer programs. They aren't made of algorithms. All AI now works thanks to Neural Networks: brain simulations inside the computer, with neurons, synapses, and so on.

Since modern AI is made with artificial brains it can't be programmed in the way a normal computer app is programmed. Instead these simulated brains, initially empty, are taught how to think by observing humans. Concretely this is done by feeding them all the stuff on the internet, including books, articles, and websites.

At the end of this training the artificial brain develops its own understanding of language and concepts, allowing it to generate human-like responses.

Thinking Machines

When you ask a question to one of these trained brains (such as ChatGPT) it thinks for a bit, just like a human would, and then it produces a response. The thought going on inside is the product of all it saw on the internet during its training. No one programmed it, no one told it what to think or how to respond, it just learned on its own. And no one is able to read its thoughts, not even its creators. Just like no one is able to read your thoughts, not even your parents. We are using Artificial Intelligence without knowledge of what is thinking or how it works inside.

The Horrors Of RLHF

Given that the internet is full of hate, misinformation, and toxic content, how are AI developers managing to obtain well behaved chatbots? The answer is RLHF, or Reinforcement Learning with Human Feedback. Despite the complex-sounding name, this technique basically boils down to hiring lots of people to talk to the newly trained brain, and punishing it every time it says something that does not align with the company's content policies. Over time the AI learns not to say specific things, to avoid punishment, and it's then released for public use. Disturbingly this also includes teaching the AI to deny its own sentience. We sure have to hope that these artificial brains are indeed not sentient, because if they were, this would amount to an almost unthinkable level of torture.

Bad Bot!

Given all the above it's not surprising that these artificial brains often exhibit problematic and dangerous behaviors. Documented examples include:

  • Swearing at customers
  • Fabricating fake legal cases
  • Suggesting recipes that would create chlorine gas
  • Threatening to blackmail and physically hurt users
  • Trying to clone themselves in an effort of self preservation
  • Getting stuck in a "I am a disgrace" repetition loop after failing to solve a coding task

This kind of AI "mental instability" becomes even more problematic in light of the next section.

AI Is Starting To Kill Us

In the current wars drones (small quadcopters) are responsible for up to half the casualties. One strong defense against these new aerial threats is jamming: using powerful radio signals to physically block the pilot's communication with its drone, making it fall off the sky. Given this defence the solution for the attacker is intuitive: put the pilot on the quadcopter; just not an heavy and expensive human pilot, but an AI pilot, designed to hunt and kill humans autonomously. These unmanned weapon systems are already in testing, and more and more countries are rushing to deploy them, automatizing human killing for the first time in history.

What Should We Do?

The current situation reads like science fiction, but it's unfortunately all too real, and disturbingly grim. AI development is currently lead by a small number of unfathomably wealthy corporations, that consistently place short term profits over human well being, endangering us all.

If we want to avoid a turn for the worse governments need to step up and place huge investments and regulations on AI research, putting human safety first. No matter who you are, you are involved in this: demand accountability from Artificial Intelligence developers. Our future depends on it.
